Impact on Swine Production

Our research translates into measurable benefits, helping producers raise healthier animals and reduce environmental load.

Production Benefits

Improved sow longevity

Fewer health issues and longer productive lives.

Enhanced reproductive performance

Higher litter size and healthier piglets, with 0.8 more piglets per litter in ou studies.

Better growth and development

Higher weaning weights and more robust post-weaning performance.

Reduced antibiotic dependency

Less antibiotic use due to microbiome focused feeding strategies.

Sustainability Advantages

Better use of fibrous byproducts

Better digestibility of co-products ingredients.

Lower environmental impact

Nitrogen and phosphorus excretion reduced in optimized feeding systems.

Reduced reliance on imported feed

More efficient use of local ingredients lowers feed costs and transportation impact.

Better feed efficiency

Improvement in feed conversion ratio through microbiome responsive diets.

Our Collaborative Approach

We believe in Responsible Research and Innovation that integrates stakeholder feedback throughout the research cycle.

Dialogue Model

We engage in mutual exchange with producers, veterinarians, and industry partners, creating space for feedback and practical insights that refine our research based on real-world constraints.

Participation Model

We involve stakeholders in co-designing trials and interpreting data, making collaborative decisions about dietary formulations, prebiotic use, and housing conditions.

Continuous Innovation Cycle

Our research integrates adoption strategies and stakeholder feedback to continually improve our innovations and ensure they address real industry needs.

Pedro Urriola

Dr. Pedro Urriola works on sustainability of pork production by studying the intersection of animal health and the environment at a systems level. We apply nutritional evaluation of agro-industrial co-products using novel techniques. In addition to feed evaluation projects, Pedro is involved in projects on the management and control of infectious diseases transmission such as African Swine Fever in his appointment and with collaborations with the College of Veterinary Medicine.
